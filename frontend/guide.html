How Parcel Scout works

A quick walkthrough of a search, start to finish.

Step 1 of 5

Start with a suburb

Type a few letters and pick from the dropdown — it suggests real Victorian suburbs and their postcode as you type, so a typo like "werribe" still gets you to the right place instead of a dead-end search.

Step 2 of 5

Narrow it down (optional)

Add a price range, land size, or restrict to private treaty vs auction only if you want a tighter shortlist — every filter is optional, and Parcel Scout searches live listings that match.

Step 3 of 5

Get a ranked, scored result list

Every matching listing comes back with a 0-100 value score, its zoning, any planning overlays, and how far it is from the nearest train station — sorted so the best-value listings are at the top.

Step 4 of 5

Read the value score

The score blends location (established vs greenfield, distance to the nearest station) with how the price compares to recent sold comparables in that suburb. 50 means "priced right at the suburb median" — not bad, just average. A great score needs to be strong on both location and price, not just one.

85 Strong buy — good location and price
55 Average — priced about right
30 Weak — overpriced and/or poor location
Step 5 of 5

Check zoning & overlays before you inspect

Every result shows its base zoning and any planning overlays pulled straight from Victoria's official planning data — heritage, bushfire, flood risk and more — so you know about a real constraint before you book an inspection, not after.
